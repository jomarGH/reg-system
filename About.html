System Overview

The Word Journey The Word Journey is a guided path through the 73 books of the Catholic Bible, helping disciples faithfully record their readings, track their growth in chapters and verses, and reflect on their progress through a clear and purposeful completion system.

Core Philosophy: A book is considered "Fully Completed" only when the reader has finished ALL chapters of that book. This encourages deep, consistent engagement with God's Word.

The Word Journey provides each disciple with a personal dashboard to reflect on their spiritual progress, an Admin Analytics Panel to steward and guide the community, and a Leaderboard that fosters encouragement, unity, and a shared pursuit of growth in God’s Word..

Purpose

This system is designed to help Disciples track their Bible reading journey across all 73 Catholic books. It promotes:

  • Consistency — Daily reading habits through visual progress tracking
  • Accountability — Public leaderboards and admin oversight
  • Completion — Encourages finishing entire books, not just selected passages
  • Community — Families can share a mobile number while maintaining individual progress

How It Works

  • 📱 Registration: Disciples register using their mobile number. Family members can share one number with individual profiles.
  • 📖 Recording Progress: Each reading session is recorded in the system (Book, Chapters Read, Verses Read, Completion Date).
  • 📊 Progress Calculation: The system aggregates all entries per book. A book is marked "Fully Completed" when total chapters read ≥ required chapters (e.g., Genesis needs 50 chapters).
  • 👑 Leaderboard: Rankings are based on fully completed books and total chapters read.

Book Completion Logic (Accurate)

How it works: The system aggregates all progress entries for a specific book and sums the chapters read. If the total meets or exceeds the book's total chapter count, the book is marked "FULLY COMPLETED".
Example
  • Genesis requires 50 chapters
  • User reads Genesis 50 chapters → Save
  • Total = 50 chapters → ✅ FULLY COMPLETED
Why this matters: Traditional trackers count one entry as "book done" regardless of chapter completion. Our logic ensures integrity and aligns with real reading achievements.

This logic applies to all 73 Catholic books, from Genesis (50 chapters) to Revelation (22 chapters), including Psalms (150 chapters) and Isaiah (66 chapters).
